Project Overview

Welcome to the Anglican Churches of Jamaica digital archive, a comprehensive initiative dedicated to documenting, preserving, and sharing the rich history of over 350 Anglican churches across the island.

This project serves as a vital resource for historians, researchers, students, and anyone interested in Jamaica's religious heritage and architectural history.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission

To create a comprehensive digital archive that preserves the history, architecture, and cultural significance of Jamaica's Anglican churches, making this valuable heritage accessible to people worldwide.

Vision

To become the definitive resource for Anglican church history in Jamaica, supporting preservation efforts, academic research, and public education about this important aspect of Jamaican culture.

Historical Significance

Anglican churches in Jamaica represent more than just places of worship‚Äîthey are living monuments to the island's complex history, from the colonial era through emancipation and into modern times.

Architectural Heritage

These churches showcase a range of architectural styles including Georgian, Gothic Revival, and Victorian, reflecting the different periods of Jamaican history and the influences that shaped the island.

Cultural Importance

Many of these churches served as community centers, educational institutions, and landmarks that have witnessed key moments in Jamaica's development as a nation.
